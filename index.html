J
Joule — SAP's Generative AI Copilot
Universal copilot for navigating and working inside SAP applications using natural language
▼
Where available

SAP S/4HANA Public & Private Editions, SuccessFactors, SAP Sports One, SAP Start, SAP Fieldglass, SAP Build Code, SAP BTP Cockpit, SAP HANA Cloud, and more. Depth of integration and available actions vary by module and release.

Strengths
  • Universal copilot for navigating, automating, and extracting insights from SAP applications using natural language
  • Context-aware query responses that understand the user's current SAP context
  • Deeply integrated with SAP UI and data structures — not a generic chatbot overlay
  • Reduces support ticket volume for repetitive navigation and task queries
  • Eases the learning curve for occasional or infrequent SAP users
Weaknesses
  • Full feature set requires explicit enablement — not available by default in all environments
  • May be subject to additional licensing depending on deployment
  • Early-stage or beta in some solutions — feature scope varies by beta/GA status
  • Capability depth is uneven across modules; some have deep Joule integration, others have limited actions
Limitations
  • Depth of integration and available actions vary by module and release; do not assume consistent behaviour across all SAP products
  • Data privacy and security implications must be assessed per deployment, particularly where generative features process business data
  • Enablement, release status, and governance must be confirmed before positioning to customers
Best for
Getting help inside SAP applications Natural-language task guidance Navigation support for occasional users Reducing repetitive support queries
Not ideal for
Assuming identical capability across every SAP product Deployments where enablement & governance have not been verified Complex analytical work requiring deep expert reasoning
⬡
SAP AI Core & Generative AI Hub
Foundation layer and developer platform for orchestrating, managing, and deploying AI/ML models on SAP BTP
▼
Where available

Foundation layer for SAP AI features. Available as Generative AI Hub within SAP Business Technology Platform (SAP BTP). This is the underlying infrastructure that powers SAP Business AI capabilities more broadly.

Strengths
  • Enables orchestration, monitoring, and lifecycle management of AI/ML models at scale
  • Plug-and-play support for various AI building blocks and model types
  • Connects to a broad range of SAP data sources and business processes
  • Provides governance and observability for AI deployments
  • Supports both SAP-native models and external LLM integrations via Generative AI Hub
  • Central place for experimenting with multiple AI models under controlled, governed conditions
  • Strong fit for demo environments and custom LLM showcase scenarios
  • Useful for building and orchestrating custom AI scenarios — including multi-model and multi-agent setups
  • Supports governance, privacy, security controls, and controlled experimentation in SAP-managed infrastructure
Weaknesses
  • Intended for technically capable users — developers, architects, and IT teams
  • Not direct end-user tooling; provides no out-of-the-box business UX
  • Foundation layer rather than a complete, deployable business solution
  • Requires understanding of AI/ML concepts, BTP services, and integration patterns
Limitations
  • Requires significant technical capability and setup effort before delivering value
  • Not appropriate for someone simply seeking in-app AI assistance
  • Pricing, tiers, and available services subject to BTP licensing and regional availability
  • Requires familiarity with SAP BTP and orchestration concepts; better suited to technical builders than casual end users
Best for
Developers and IT teams AI/ML model orchestration and lifecycle management Building custom AI-enabled applications on BTP Technical AI foundations and governance Model experimentation Controlled LLM access Technical demo backends Custom AI scenario orchestration
Not ideal for
Non-technical business users seeking simple in-app guidance Out-of-the-box business workflow automation without development effort
📄
AI Document Processing — SAP Document AI
Automated extraction and processing of information from semi-structured and unstructured business documents
▼
Capabilities covered

Document information extraction, AI-assisted sales order processing, quality certificate processing, hotel receipt processing, goods receipt processing. Available as both embedded and standalone models depending on the deployment context.

Strengths
  • Highly accurate extraction from semi-structured or unstructured documents when properly configured
  • Supports both embedded (within SAP solutions) and standalone deployment models
  • Automates repetitive document handling that otherwise requires manual data entry
  • Covers a range of common document types: receipts, purchase orders, certificates, goods receipts
Weaknesses
  • Requires initial setup and tuning — not zero-effort out of the box
  • Accuracy is sensitive to document layout consistency; highly variable formats reduce performance
  • Some features are still in development or require premium/licensed tiers
  • Edge cases and unusual document variants may need manual review workflows
Limitations
  • Accuracy and business value depend heavily on document consistency and the quality of initial configuration
  • Premium or advanced feature tiers may carry additional licensing requirements
  • Not a substitute for human review in high-stakes or highly variable document scenarios without adequate tuning
Best for
Document intake automation Extraction from receipts, orders, and certificates Reducing manual data entry in finance and procurement High-volume, consistent document flows
Not ideal for
Zero-setup deployments without configuration investment Highly inconsistent or freeform document formats without tuning Scenarios requiring 100% automation without human-in-the-loop review
✍
AI-Assisted Text and Writing Tools
Draft assistance, text generation, and feedback support embedded in HR, performance, and procurement workflows
▼
Where available

SAP SuccessFactors (Goals, Calibration, Recruiting, 360 Reviews), SAP Fieldglass, SAP Enable Now, SAP LeanIX, and more. Subject to enablement and licensing per solution and version.

Strengths
  • Draft assistance — helps users produce first drafts faster in structured workflows
  • Writing corrections and tone suggestions within HR and procurement contexts
  • Goal generation in SuccessFactors — suggests SMART-style goal language
  • Feedback and comment recommendations in calibration and 360 review flows
  • Increases productivity and consistency in repetitive writing tasks across HR, performance management, and sourcing
Weaknesses
  • May generate generic or under-contextualised text if the user provides minimal context in their prompt
  • Good outcomes depend on thoughtful prompting and clear context definition
  • Cannot substitute for human judgement in performance decisions or policy interpretation
Limitations
  • Output quality depends directly on the quality of context and user guidance provided
  • Output should be reviewed and edited; treat it as a first draft, not a final product
  • Some features may require premium access, specific SuccessFactors modules, or explicit enablement by admins
  • Not all writing tools are available across all SAP solutions — coverage varies by product
Best for
Draft generation in HR and procurement workflows Performance feedback and comment assistance Goal-writing support in SuccessFactors Structured writing tasks in familiar SAP contexts
Not ideal for
Expecting deep expert judgment without user review Vague prompts expecting strong outputs Final-form content without editing
💬
Conversational AI Agents & Assistants
Chatbots and digital assistants for help-desk support, process guidance, and simple workflow queries
▼
Examples and coverage

SAP Digital Assistant, chatbots integrated for HR policy, procurement, and IT topics, community GenAI chatbot. Available in areas including SuccessFactors, SAP Start, SAP Sports One, incentives management, and more. Coverage varies by deployment.

Strengths
  • Automates common help-desk inquiries, reducing load on HR, IT, and procurement support teams
  • Handles basic process guidance — "how do I submit a leave request", "where do I find my payslip"
  • Available across multiple SAP solution areas with growing coverage
  • Reduces time-to-answer for frequently asked questions
Weaknesses
  • Best suited to transactional or simple queries — limited ability for deep problem-solving, though this is improving
  • Quality varies significantly depending on how well the underlying knowledge base or intent model has been configured
  • Not expert-level reasoning; cannot reliably handle nuanced policy interpretation or exception handling
Limitations
  • Not suited for complex troubleshooting or cases that require deep contextual reasoning
  • Better for common, well-defined workflows than edge-case diagnosis or ambiguous queries
  • GenAI-powered variants are still maturing; accuracy and confidence can vary
Best for
FAQ automation Help-desk style support reduction Simple process and navigation guidance First-line employee self-service
Not ideal for
Complex troubleshooting with multiple dependencies Nuanced advisory work requiring deep business context Handling policy exceptions or edge cases reliably

Domain-Specific AI Agents & Features
Embedded AI capabilities across supply chain, procurement, analytics, sustainability, and solution-specific workflows
▼

This category covers embedded AI capabilities within specific SAP solutions and domains. Maturity, availability, and licensing vary significantly by product, version, and region. Each sub-area below summarises what exists and what to be aware of.

Sub-areas
Supply Chain & Manufacturing Embedded AI
  • Examples: Visual inspection (Digital Manufacturing), intelligent goods receipt, transportation management, predictive labour demand planning
  • Strengths: Raises inspection accuracy, automates visual and manual checks, supports operational optimisation across production and logistics
  • Limitations: Quality depends on training data quality and process customisation; may require extended deployment and configuration effort
Procurement & Spend Management Embedded AI
  • Examples: Bid analysis, expense automation agents, request assistants (SAP Concur, Ariba, Fieldglass)
  • Strengths: Speed and accuracy in itemisation, categorisation, and supplier segmentation; reduces manual spend analysis effort
  • Limitations: Premium features may require subscription or additional licensing; coverage varies by region and product version
Analytics Embedded AI
  • Examples: Natural-language analytics (Just Ask in SAP Analytics Cloud), NL2SQL in HANA Cloud
  • Strengths: Enables non-expert users to query analytical data using plain language; reduces dependency on technical report writers
  • Limitations: Domain-specific jargon or ambiguous queries may yield inconsistent results; still maturing — validate output before relying on it for decisions
Sustainability & EHS Embedded AI
  • Examples: Automated emission factor mapping, safety observation reporting, compliance management
  • Strengths: Reduces manual effort in compliance data capture and sustainability reporting workflows
  • Limitations: Requires ongoing alignment with regulatory and reporting frameworks that evolve frequently; not a substitute for qualified compliance expertise
Solution-Embedded AI Highlights By product
  • SuccessFactors: Job description generation, interview question generation, career insights, 360 review AI, calibration AI
  • Service/Sales Cloud v2: Email draft suggestions, lead booster, survey summaries
  • SAP Concur: Hotel receipt itemisation, request automation
  • SAP Ariba: AI-driven category management
  • SAP Signavio: Process mining with AI recommendations, process explanation, performance recommender
  • SAP LeanIX: Inventory prompt, AI-generated context, translation support
Embedded AI capabilities vary significantly by solution, version, licensing, and maturity. Always check the current release notes and enablement requirements for the specific product before relying on a particular feature being available.
Best for
Vertical and domain-specific workflow automation Operations teams working within established SAP solutions Reducing manual effort in highly repetitive domain tasks
Not ideal for
Assuming uniform availability across all regions and versions Deployments without confirmed licensing and enablement Replacing domain expertise in high-stakes compliance or analytical decisions
Demo & build

Demo & prototyping tools for SAP employees

This section covers tools that employees may use to build demos, prototypes, and showcase scenarios. These are different from embedded SAP product features — they are about build velocity and creative assembly. Approved and compliant usage should be verified for your specific environment.

BC
SAP Build Code & Vibe Coding
AI-supported full-stack prototype and application creation for SAP BTP, SAPUI5, and Fiori-based workflows
▼
What it is

AI-supported full-stack application and prototype creation from natural language, directly supporting BTP, SAPUI5, Fiori Elements, and related developer workflows — including extensions in Visual Studio Code. Enables "vibe coding" for rapid SAP-centric scaffolding without starting from scratch.

Strengths
  • Reduces development time significantly for common SAP app and extension patterns
  • Helps democratise app and demo creation for less experienced developers
  • Strong fit for quick proof-of-concepts and customer-facing prototypes
  • Can use AI-assisted code generation in SAP-centric environments
  • Supports SAP best-practice-oriented development workflows — more context-aware than general coding tools for SAP-specific patterns
Weaknesses
  • For complex custom logic or deep backend integration, SAP domain knowledge is still needed
  • Productivity gains depend on the user's technical capability and prompt quality
  • Best for acceleration — not a replacement for engineering judgment in complex scenarios
Limitations
  • Best for acceleration, not magic — deep custom logic and enterprise-grade architecture still require engineering judgment
  • Generated code should be reviewed before use in customer environments
Best for
Rapid prototyping Full-stack demo builds SAP-centric application scaffolding Quick PoCs for BTP, SAPUI5, or Fiori
Not ideal for
Assuming AI can replace SAP technical understanding Highly complex backend architecture with no domain expertise

AI Coding Agents & Tools
Claude Code, GitHub Copilot, Cline, Cursor, and similar agentic coding tools for rapid prototyping and code generation
▼
What it covers

AI-assisted coding tools — including Claude Code, GitHub Copilot, Cline, Cursor, and comparable agentic coding environments — used for rapid prototyping, code generation, refactoring, spec-to-prototype work, dashboard creation, DevOps scripting, design-to-code, and related build tasks. These are general-purpose tools that work well across a wide range of engineering contexts.

Strengths
  • Extremely fast for experimentation and prototype velocity
  • Reduces manual coding effort across a wide range of languages and frameworks
  • Helps lower the barrier to entry for building demos and functional prototypes
  • Strong fit for Visual Studio Code-based workflows
  • Useful across coding, product, and operational tasks — not limited to SAP contexts
Weaknesses
  • General-purpose tools: may not be as SAP-context-aware as SAP-native tools for SAP-specific patterns
  • Quality depends on prompting, review, and integration context
  • Connection to SAP-specific data sources and standards depends on how the tool is configured and used
Limitations
  • Responsible and compliant use is required — availability and approved usage should be verified for the employee's specific environment
  • These tools are excellent for general coding; Joule and SAP-native tooling are stronger for SAP-specific business context
  • Output should be reviewed; agentic tools can make sweeping changes that require validation
Best for
Rapid coding and prototyping Refactoring and code generation Turning specs into working demo assets quickly DevOps scripting and automation
Not ideal for
Assuming deep SAP business/process context automatically Using without review, validation, or internal compliance checks
Practical rule: use SAP-native tools when SAP context matters most; use coding agents when build speed and general engineering productivity matter most.

Decision path

How the tools fit together

A practical decision path — not a strict architecture stack — for choosing the right tool for the job.

1
Need in-app help or embedded SAP AI?
Use: Joule, writing tools, conversational assistants, embedded AI features
2
Need to build a custom prototype or app?
Use: SAP Build Code
3
Need model orchestration or multi-model experimentation?
Use: SAP AI Core / Generative AI Hub
4
Need very fast code generation or prototype acceleration?
Use: Claude Code / GitHub Copilot / agentic coding tools
5
Need a cross-ecosystem Microsoft + SAP showcase?
Use: Microsoft Copilot + Joule integration scenarios
Note: This is not a strict architecture stack. It is a practical decision path for choosing the right tool for the job. Tools can be combined, and the boundaries are not rigid.

RFP guidance

Which tools should you use to help answer RFPs?

From the tools in this guide, here is what is actually useful when you are sitting down to write or respond to an RFP — and what is not built for that job.

✅ Useful for answering RFPs
rfp.io (SAP-integrated)
SAP's purpose-built RFP response platform. Designed specifically for structured bid and proposal workflows within the SAP ecosystem. If your team has access, this should be your first stop before general-purpose tools.
AI Coding Agents (Claude Code, GitHub Copilot, Cline, Cursor)
The most practical tools for RFP drafting. Use them to draft, structure, summarise, and refine written responses — they are fast, flexible, and work across any document format. Best for turning bullet points into polished prose, summarising source materials, and iterating on language quickly.
Microsoft Copilot (via M365)
Useful if you are writing RFP responses in Word or working across Teams and Outlook. Can draft and refine text inline, summarise documents, and pull context from your M365 environment. Good fit if your RFP workflow lives in Office.
Joule
Useful for quickly looking up SAP product information, navigating to the right module details, and answering "how does X work in SAP" questions while you write. Not a drafting tool — but good for fact-checking and research within SAP applications.
SAP AI Core / Generative AI Hub
If your team has built an internal RFP assistant or knowledge retrieval tool on top of Generative AI Hub, that would be the strongest option — purpose-built with SAP content. Worth checking if your team has something like this available.
🚫 Not built for RFP work
AI-Assisted Writing Tools (SuccessFactors, Fieldglass, etc.)
These are scoped to HR and procurement workflows inside specific SAP solutions — goal writing, performance feedback, job descriptions. Not general-purpose writing tools and not accessible outside those modules.
Conversational AI Agents & Assistants
Built for help-desk and FAQ automation. Not designed for drafting or composing longer documents like RFP responses.
SAP Document AI
Extracts structured data from documents like receipts and purchase orders. Could theoretically parse an incoming RFP document, but that is not its designed use case and it is not a drafting or writing tool.
SAP Build Code & Domain-Specific AI
Build Code is for prototyping applications. Domain-specific AI features are embedded in operational workflows. Neither is relevant to writing RFP responses.
Bottom line: If your team has access to rfp.io, start there — it is purpose-built for this. Otherwise, use Claude Code, GitHub Copilot, or Microsoft Copilot to do the writing. Use Joule to look things up inside SAP while you work. If your team has an internal tool built on Generative AI Hub with SAP-specific content, that is also a strong option.
Important considerations

Overall limitations and considerations

Before positioning any SAP AI capability to a customer or stakeholder, review these cross-cutting considerations.

⚠️

Read before advising or positioning

  • Many features covered in this guide are in Beta, Early Adopter Care (EAC), or subject to future general availability. Do not assume current public availability without checking the latest release notes.
  • Coverage, integration depth, and business impact vary significantly across SAP products and geographic regions. What applies in one solution or market may not apply in another.
  • Data privacy implications, licensing requirements, enablement prerequisites, and technical dependencies must be assessed and confirmed on a case-by-case basis for each deployment.
  • Continued alignment with SAP product roadmaps is necessary because AI capabilities evolve rapidly — features, integrations, and commercial terms change across releases.
⚡

Extra cautions for coding and demo tools

  • Approved and compliant usage of external coding and demo tools should be verified for your specific environment — availability is not always the same across teams or regions.
  • External coding tools and integrations may require specific setup or internal approval paths; do not assume default access.
  • SAP-native tools are generally stronger for SAP-specific context; general coding agents are generally stronger for fast build velocity and prototype acceleration. These are complementary, not interchangeable.
  • Demo readiness does not always mean production readiness — prototypes built quickly with AI coding tools require proper engineering review before use in customer-facing or regulated environments.
